# Context Card

A bounded context is where terminology has stable meaning.

- **Context ID:** …
- **Scope:** What is inside? What is outside?
- **Stakeholders:** …
- **Primary concerns:** (safety, performance, correctness, cost, governance, etc.)
- **Non-goals:** …
- **Key vocabulary:** canonical terms and definitions
- **Overloaded words to avoid:** (e.g., “process”, “model”)
- **Standards / acceptance tests:** what counts as “correct” here
- **Interfaces / boundaries:** APIs, protocols, dependencies
- **Invariants:** properties that must remain true
- **Evidence expectations:** what kind of evidence is considered strong here
- **Bridges:** links to other contexts and the mapping constraints
